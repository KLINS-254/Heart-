She came like a quiet riddle at dusk,
her eyes deep as old proverbs,
where truth hides behind beauty
and wisdom watches in silence.

Her petite form held a gentle power,
like a small flame that still commands the dark,
teaching my heart that strength
does not need to be loud to be real.

Her lips were soft promises,
sealed with mystery, shaped by longing,
reminding me that some words
are felt before they are spoken.

She cared for me without ceremony;
and when the cold found me below,
from the balcony she lowered her hoodie
a silent parable of love reaching down.

So tell me, do you truly love me?
For you leave my mind divided
certain like faith, uncertain like dreams,
confusing me in the most beautiful way.
