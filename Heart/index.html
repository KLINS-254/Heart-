I met her,
and her smile shattered the silence of my soul.
It was not just a curve of lips
it was the sun breaking chains of night,
the gentle fire that made me believe
in beauty that saves.

Her eyes…
O those eyes
like quiet oceans that pull me into their depth.
When I look, I drown willingly,
and even in drowning, I live.
Her face is a scripture of softness,
each line carved by angels,
each glance a prayer answered.

No day dares pass
without her walking through my thoughts.
I carry her in the marrow of my bones,
in the hush of my breath,
in the rhythm of every silent cry.

Yet I walk through storms alone.
I crave her hug
crave it until my chest trembles.
Tears rise, not as weakness,
but as rivers of longing that cannot be dammed.
I whisper into the night,
“Come, hold me,
let your arms be the refuge
where my pain learns to sleep.”

I love her
with the violence of fire,
with the tenderness of rain.
Her beauty is not skin alone;
it is eternity dressed in flesh.
Eyes that command the sky,
a face that softens even grief,
a presence that could heal
a thousand broken yesterdays.

So I cry,
so I crave,
so I wait
for her hug to descend like mercy,
to seal the day before it dies,
to remind me that love is not a promise deferred
but an embrace fulfilled.

Until then,
I am the storm aching for her calm,
the fire begging for her rain,
the man who loves her
beyond the reach of words.
